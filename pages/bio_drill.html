    LIFE IN SHORT

    1995

    Born in Chicago

    Keith Farrelle Cozart was born on August 15, 1995, in Chicago's South Side, in the Washington Park neighborhood that would shape his music and identity.

    2011

    Rise to Fame

    At just 16, Chief Keef's raw talent caught attention with tracks like "Bang" and "3Hunna", establishing him as a voice of Chicago's streets and the drill movement.

    2012

    I Don't Like Goes Viral

    The song "I Don't Like" exploded globally, leading to a remix with Kanye West, Pusha T, Big Sean, and Jadakiss. This breakthrough secured a major label deal with Interscope Records.

    2012

    Finally Rich Released

    His debut album "Finally Rich" introduced drill music to the world, featuring hits like "Love Sosa" and "Hate Bein' Sober" that became anthems of a generation.

    2015

    Independent Era Begins

    Chief Keef launched his own label, Glory Boyz Entertainment (later Glo Gang), taking full creative control and continuing to innovate his sound.

    2023

    Legacy Cemented

    Over a decade into his career, Chief Keef's influence on hip-hop is undeniable, with countless artists citing him as an inspiration and drill music dominating global charts.
